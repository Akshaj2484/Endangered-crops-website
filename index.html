Protecting the World's Endangered Crops

Preserving Crop Diversity

What Are Endangered Crops?

Endangered crops are rare or traditional plant varieties disappearing because of climate change, monocropping, and loss of seed-saving traditions. Losing these crops means losing unique flavours, cultural heritage, and genetic traits essential for future food resilience.

Examples of Endangered Crops

Finger Millet (Ragi) — India

A nutritious traditional grain declining due to modern crop dominance.

Amaranth — South America

A once‑important ancient crop now at risk due to monoculture farming.

Sorghum Varieties — Africa

Drought‑resistant varieties disappearing from traditional farms.

Bambara Groundnut — Africa

A climate‑resilient crop at risk due to low commercial demand.

Why Are Crops Becoming Endangered?

  • Climate change & drought
  • Industrial farming & monoculture
  • Loss of cultural farming practices
  • Pests, diseases, polluted soil
  • Low market value for farmers
