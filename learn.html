Preserving Crop Diversity

Tips for Supporting Crop Diversity

  • Grow traditional or heirloom varieties in your garden
  • Buy from local farmers preserving rare crops
  • Save seeds from your own crops for future planting
  • Educate others about the importance of crop diversity

Resources

  • Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO)
  • Global Seed Vault
  • International Center for Tropical Agriculture

Examples of Endangered Crops

Some rare crops include Finger Millet (India), Amaranth (South America), Sorghum varieties (Africa), and Bambara Groundnut (Africa). Supporting these crops helps maintain biodiversity and sustainable farming practices worldwide.
