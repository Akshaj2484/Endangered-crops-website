Threats to Seed Diversity

Preserving Crop Diversity

Climate Change

Extreme weather, droughts, floods, and rising temperatures can destroy crops and reduce available seed varieties.

Monoculture Farming

Planting only one type of crop reduces genetic diversity and makes crops more vulnerable to pests and diseases.

Loss of Traditional Knowledge

Farmers and communities often have unique methods for saving and preserving seeds. Losing this knowledge puts rare varieties at risk.

Pests, Diseases, and Pollution

  • Crops can be wiped out by new pests or diseases
  • Soil contamination affects seed quality
  • Invasive species compete with traditional crops
